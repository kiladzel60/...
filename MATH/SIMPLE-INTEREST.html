General Information About Simple Interest

Simple interest fundamentals: calculate interest using principal, rate, and time

What Is Simple Interest?

Simple interest is interest calculated only on the principal amount of a loan or deposit. It does not compound; the interest for each period is based solely on the original principal.

Simple interest is commonly used for short-term loans, certain bonds, and basic interest calculations.

Key Formula

Simple Interest (SI)

SI = P × r × t
where P = principal, r = annual rate (decimal), t = time (years)

Total Amount (A)

A = P + SI = P × (1 + r × t)
Example: If you invest $1,000 at 5% per year for 3 years (simple interest):
SI = 1000 × 0.05 × 3 = 150
Total = 1000 + 150 = 1,150

Solving for Unknowns

Find Principal (P)

P = SI ÷ (r × t)
Example: Interest earned $180 at 6% for 2 years: P = 180 ÷ (0.06 × 2) = 180 ÷ 0.12 = 1,500

Find Rate (r)

r = SI ÷ (P × t)
Example: $90 interest on $600 for 3 years: r = 90 ÷ (600 × 3) = 90 ÷ 1800 = 0.05 = 5%

Find Time (t)

t = SI ÷ (P × r)
Example: $200 interest on $2,000 at 4%: t = 200 ÷ (2000 × 0.04) = 200 ÷ 80 = 2.5 years

Common Variations

  • Different time units: If time is given in months, convert to years: t (years) = months ÷ 12
  • Rate as percentage: Convert percent to decimal before using formula (e.g., 7% → 0.07)
  • Short-term loans: Many short loans use simple interest for ease of calculation

Real-World Applications

  • Personal loans: Some lenders charge simple interest for short-term borrowing
  • Bonds & bills: Certain fixed-income instruments use simple interest
  • Insurance and fees: Easy estimates for prorated interest amounts
  • Quick estimates: Use simple interest for approximate growth over short periods

Quick Reference

Variable Meaning
P Principal (initial amount)
r Annual interest rate (decimal)
t Time period in years
SI Simple Interest = P × r × t
A Total Amount = P + SI

Tips

  • Convert rates: Always convert percentages to decimals before using the formula
  • Match time units: Ensure the rate period and time units align (e.g., annual rate with years)
  • Short vs long term: Use simple interest for short durations; use compound interest for longer-term growth
