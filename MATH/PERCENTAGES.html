General Information About Percentages

Understanding Percentages: A Foundation for Mathematics & Real-World Applications

What is a Percentage?

A percentage is a number or ratio expressed as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" comes from the Latin phrase "per centum," which means "by the hundred." Percentages are denoted by the symbol % and are used to express how large or small one quantity is relative to another.

Percentages are fundamental in mathematics and are used extensively in everyday life, from calculating discounts in stores to understanding statistics in news reports.

Basic Concepts

Understanding the Notation

  • 25% = 25/100 = 0.25
  • 50% = 50/100 = 0.5
  • 100% = 100/100 = 1 (whole)
  • 150% = 150/100 = 1.5 (more than the whole)

Key Terminology

  • Percentage: The number with the % symbol (e.g., 25%)
  • Decimal Form: The percentage written as a decimal (e.g., 0.25)
  • Fraction Form: The percentage written as a fraction (e.g., 1/4)

Percentage Formulas

Finding a Percentage of a Number

Percentage Value = (Percentage ÷ 100) × Base Number
or
Part = (Percentage/100) × Whole
Example: What is 30% of 200?
Value = (30/100) × 200 = 0.3 × 200 = 60

Finding What Percentage One Number Is of Another

Percentage = (Part ÷ Whole) × 100
Example: What percentage is 45 of 150?
Percentage = (45/150) × 100 = 0.3 × 100 = 30%

Finding the Base (Whole) When Given Percentage and Part

Whole = Part ÷ (Percentage ÷ 100)
or
Base = Part / (Percentage/100)
Example: 60 is 40% of what number?
Whole = 60 ÷ (40/100) = 60 ÷ 0.4 = 150

Percentage Change

Calculating Percentage Increase or Decrease

Percentage Change = ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100
Increase Example: A price increased from $50 to $65
Percentage Change = ((65 - 50) / 50) × 100 = (15/50) × 100 = 30% increase
Decrease Example: A price decreased from $80 to $60
Percentage Change = ((60 - 80) / 80) × 100 = (-20/80) × 100 = -25% decrease

Tips for Working with Percentages

  • Convert First: Convert percentages to decimals before performing calculations
  • Identify the Whole: Always know what the "100%" represents
  • Use Formula Method: Apply the appropriate formula based on what you're solving for
  • Check Your Work: Verify results make sense in context
  • Practice Common Values: Memorize common percentages like 25%, 50%, and 75%
